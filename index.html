Acknowledgement

We acknowledge the Whadjuk Noongar people as the Traditional Owners of Wadjemup, their continued connection to the island and its waters, and pay our respects to elders past, present and future.

We pay our respect to all Aboriginal people affected by the historical use of Wadjemup as a place of unjust incarceration, especially the men and boys who still remain there, and to the families and descendants who lost them.

Wadjemup Wirin Bidi Commemorative Ceremony

The Whadjuk Noongar people are the Traditional Owners of Wadjemup / Rottnest Island, and their connection to the island is long, significant, and enduring.

However, for nearly a century, Wadjemup was used as a place of incarceration, segregation and forced labour for over 4,000 Aboriginal men and boys who were forcibly taken from regions across Western Australia – Southwest Noongar areas comprising of Whadjuk, Ballardong, Yued, Karri Karrak, Gnaala Karla Booja and Wagyl Kaip, and regions comprising of Yamatji, Goldfields, Pilbara, Kimberley, and the Western and Central Deserts.

Wadjemup Wirin Bidi, meaning ‘Spirit Trail’ in the Whadjuk Noongar language, is a public commemorative ceremony for Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal people to gather together to acknowledge and pay respect to those Aboriginal men and boys, including those who remain on Wadjemup.

Attendance is free but registration is required.

Program

The public commemorative ceremony will be held on Wadjemup on Saturday 9 November 2024, with truth-telling from Traditional Owners and Elders from around Western Australia, Aboriginal performances, yarning circles, arts and crafts, and children’s activities.

While every intent is for events to go ahead as planned, in some circumstances they may have to change due to inclement weather or other unforeseen circumstances.

Where is the event located?

Wadjemup Wirin Bidi will take place on Wadjemup / Rottnest Island.

The commemorative ceremony will be held at Wadjemup Oval, which is 10-minute walk or 750 metres from the Main Jetty, along flat pathways. Access is via Strue Road.

Wadjemup Museum is located close to the Main Jetty, and access is via Digby Drive.

Travel to Wadjemup

Travel to Wadjemup is not included in your event registration, so please remember to make arrangements in advance.

Travelling by ferry is the most popular way to reach the island. There are three ferry operators to choose from, with multiple departure points:

  • Perth City (Barrack Street Jetty): Up to 90 minutes travel time
  • North Fremantle: Approximately 25 minutes travel time
  • Fremantle: Approximately 25 minutes travel time
  • Hillarys Boat Harbour: Approximately 45 minutes travel time

For current schedules and to book your transfer, please contact your preferred ferry provider:

  • Rottnest Express
  • SeaLink Rottnest
  • Rottnest Fast Ferries

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I register?

You can register to attend this free event by following the prompts online here. Please ensure you register everyone attending with you, including children.

Participants of all ages are welcome and dedicated children’s activities will be on offer, but parental guidance is encouraged for young audiences.

Yes, drinking water and other refreshments will be available. There will also be food and drink available for purchase.

We acknowledge that attendees may find it distressing to be on Wadjemup. Cultural care services will be available.

No, ferry travel is not included in your event registration. Please remember to book your ferry tickets in advance.

Will there be security at the ceremony?

There will be appropriate security to ensure the safety of all attendees.
